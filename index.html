Research

Selected research

01

Two-component backfill grout for shield TBM tail void

Master's thesis. Characterized grout properties — flow, gel time, plasticity, compressive strength, bleeding, viscosity — and evaluated injection performance (watertightness, fillability, homogeneity, soundness) on a 1:8 scaled tunnel model, proposing quantitative criteria for shield-TBM backfilling.

Model Testing · Injection Performance · KEPCORead the thesis (PDF) ↓
02

Clay–bacteria interaction

Sedimentation and consolidation tests with kaolin and Shewanella oneidensis MR-1, revealing how microbial interaction densifies sediments — pathways for soil remediation and sediment stabilization.

Sedimentation · Consolidation
03

CO₂-treated recycled aggregates

Carbonation treatment improved recycled-aggregate performance — crushing value reduced to 30.3% and CBR raised to 137.7% — while lowering leachate pH and chromium, advancing sustainable construction materials.

Carbonation · Sustainability · KEPCO
04

Clay-mixed MICP for rock-mass sealing

Developing an eco-friendly grouting method that uses clay-mixed microbially induced calcite precipitation (MICP) to seal jointed rock masses — reducing reliance on cement-based grouts.

MICP · Bio-grouting · NRF
